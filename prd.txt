# Feature Roadmap: Grounded in Narasimha Rao's Textbook

I read through the book's structure (515 pages, 37 chapters across 6 parts) and sampled several key computational chapters — Arudha Padas, Divisional Charts, Upagrahas, Yogas, Avasthas, Vimshottari Dasa, Sudarsana Chakra Dasa, Mundane Astrology. This is not a typical astrology book. **It's a computational manual** — nearly every concept comes with precise formulas, worked examples, and exercises. That is extraordinarily valuable because it means every chapter maps cleanly to a software feature.

Here's the critical insight that changes your positioning: **each of these calculations is a mathematical procedure, not a language output**. This is the exact moat against the "ChatGPT can do this" objection. ChatGPT cannot compute Sarvashtakavarga bindus, cannot cast a Navamsa chart, cannot determine the stronger co-lord of Aquarius using Narasimha Rao's multi-step tiebreaker rules. Your product can.

## The feature architecture: three layers

Think of the product as three layers stacked on top of each other:

**Layer 1 — Calculation Engine (your moat).** Pure math, deterministic, auditable. This is what ChatGPT cannot do and will never do.

**Layer 2 — Interpretive Library (your content asset).** Structured rulesets encoding what the book teaches — yoga definitions, dasa interpretations, avastha results, house significations. When a yoga fires, the interpretation is rule-based first, LLM-enhanced second.

**Layer 3 — Narrative/AI Layer (your UX polish).** DeepSeek or similar generates readable prose, but grounded in Layer 1 + 2 outputs. Never hallucinating, always citing the rule that triggered.

Now the feature map, grouped by book part.

## Part 1: Core Chart Analysis — the MVP foundation

These are non-negotiable. Without them, serious users will dismiss the product immediately.

**Rasi chart engine** with Swiss Ephemeris backend, producing the natal chart in North Indian, South Indian, and East Indian styles. Support for multiple ayanamsas (Lahiri, Raman, KP, Krishnamurti, True Chitra) because this is a common source of user complaint in Indian astrology software.

**Full Varga (divisional chart) suite — all 20 vargas from the book:** D-1 (Rasi), D-2 (Hora), D-3 (Drekkana), D-4 (Chaturthamsa), D-7 (Saptamsa), D-9 (Navamsa), D-10 (Dasamsa), D-12 (Dwadasamsa), D-16 (Shodasamsa), D-20 (Vimsamsa), D-24 (Chaturvimsamsa), D-27 (Nakshatramsa/Bhamsa), D-30 (Trimsamsa), D-40 (Khavedamsa), D-45 (Akshavedamsa), D-60 (Shashtyamsa). Most free astrology sites give only D-1 and D-9. Giving all 20 is a differentiator by itself, especially D-60 which very few software tools compute correctly.

**Special Lagnas** — Bhava Lagna, Hora Lagna, Ghatika Lagna, Vighatika Lagna, Sree Lagna, Pranapada Lagna, Indu Lagna, Varnada Lagna. Each has a precise calculation the book specifies.

**Upagrahas** — all 11: Dhuma, Vyatipata, Parivesha, Indrachapa, Upaketu (Sun-based) and Kaala, Mrityu, Arthaprahara, Yamaghantaka, Gulika, Maandi (time-based). The book gives exact formulas for each. Most astrology software ignores these entirely — that's a feature gap you can fill.

**Arudha Padas** — Bhava Arudhas for all 12 houses in all divisional charts, plus Graha Arudhas. The book's chapter 9 treats this as foundational, but mainstream apps barely touch it. Include the exception rule for houses 1 and 7 (noted in the book: the pada must not be in the same house or the 7th from it — shift to the 10th from there if so).

**Karakas** — both Naisargika (fixed) and Chara (variable, recomputed per chart with the full ordering algorithm — 8 planets including Rahu, sorted by degrees within sign, including the reversed-longitude rule for Rahu). The book is explicit about this and most software gets the Chara Karaka order wrong.

**Aspects and Argalas** — both graha drishti (planetary aspects with their varying strengths: Mars 4/7/8, Jupiter 5/7/9, Saturn 3/7/10) and rasi drishti. Argalas (primary, secondary, and virodha/counter-argalas) on every house. Very few tools compute argalas at all.

**Yoga Detection Engine — the single highest-ROI feature.** The book covers hundreds of yogas: Raja Yogas (with Parijatamsa/Uttamsaamsa/Simhasanamsa magnitude calculation using the Dasa Varga count), Dhana Yogas, Pancha Mahapurusha Yogas (Ruchaka, Bhadra, Hamsa, Malavya, Sasa), Gaja Kesari, Neecha Bhanga, Vipareeta Raja Yogas (Harsha, Sarala, Vimala), Kemadruma, Kala Sarpa, and 200+ more. Build a rules engine where each yoga is a JSON rule specification; the engine evaluates all rules against the chart and returns which yogas fire, with citations to the book. This alone takes weeks to build but creates a feature that no ChatGPT prompt can replicate and that serious astrologers will pay for.

**Ashtakavarga** — Bhinnashtakavarga (individual planet bindus across all 12 rasis), Sarvashtakavarga (totals), Trikona Sodhana and Ekadhipatya Sodhana (the two reduction methods the book covers), Sodhya Pindas. This is arithmetic the book lays out precisely; ChatGPT cannot produce correct numbers here.

**Shadbala** — all six strength components (Sthana, Dig, Kaala, Chesta, Naisargika, Drik Bala) plus Ishta-Kashta phala. The book's Chapter 15 covers every formula. Display as a radar chart per planet — visually impressive and genuinely useful.

**Avasthas** — the three major sets: Baaladi (5 states by position in sign), Jagradadi (3 states: waking/dreaming/sleeping), and Sayanadi (12 activity states with Parasara's (C×P×A+M+G+L) mod 12 formula I saw in the book). The last one is particularly rare in software and is computationally deep.

**Longevity estimation** — the book has a whole chapter; implement Pinda, Nisargika, and Amsa methods and cross-verify. Present as a range, not a point estimate, with appropriate disclaimers.

## Part 2: Dasa Systems — predictive time-series

This is where the timeline and forecast UX gets built.

**Vimshottari Dasa** with all 5 levels: Mahadasha → Antardasha → Pratyantardasha → Sookshmadasha → Praana dasha. Render as an interactive zoomable timeline (think a horizontal scroll of the user's whole life from birth to ~120 years, drillable by zoom level). This is where the "persistent timeline" advantage I mentioned earlier comes alive.

**Ashtottari Dasa** — the 108-year alternate nakshatra dasa, applicable conditionally per the book's rules. Software should auto-detect applicability.

**Narayana Dasa** — the book calls this "the most versatile rasi dasa." Compute for rasi chart and all divisional charts. Used for timing events per specific house themes.

**Lagna Kendradi Rasi Dasa** and **Sudasa** — for material success timing (business, wealth, career events).

**Drigdasa** — for spiritual evolution timing.

**Shoola Dasa** and **Niryaana Shoola Dasa** — ayur dasas for longevity-related timing. Handle with extreme care in UX (option to disable "death timing" displays; many users shouldn't see this).

**Kalachakra Dasa** — Parasara called it "the most respectable dasa." Complex to compute (navamsa-based, deha/jeeva rasi calculations, forward/reverse motion rules) — exactly why ChatGPT cannot do it.

**Sudarsana Chakra Dasa** — 12-year cycling dasa with antardasha and pratyantardasha, grounded in Tajaka charts. Excellent for annual forecasting.

## Part 3: Transits — the daily engagement layer

This is where retention comes from. Users open the app daily for current transits.

**Daily transit dashboard** — current planetary positions, their aspects to natal planets, current Vimshottari period active, today's panchanga (tithi, nakshatra, yoga, karana, vara). Personalized for the user's chart.

**Transit timeline calendar** — a forward-looking calendar showing major transit events with exact date ranges: "Saturn enters your 7th house from March 14, retrogrades back August 10, re-enters October 22." Clickable for detailed rules-based interpretation.

**Ashtakavarga-based transit analysis** — when a planet transits through a sign with high bindus for the native, it's favorable. This is a precise numerical judgment the book teaches.

**Sodhya Pinda transits** — advanced transit analysis the book covers that virtually no consumer software offers.

**Nakshatra-based transits** — Tara Chakra, Vedha concepts, planetary friendship with nakshatra lords. The book has detailed rules.

**Gochara journal (my favorite sticky feature)** — the app logs each day's major transits; user adds a one-line note on what actually happened. After 6 months, the user has a personal correlation database. This is completely impossible on ChatGPT (stateless) and creates real lock-in.

**Monthly and sixty-hour charts** — the book's Chapter 27 covers both.

**Birthtime Rectification tool.** The book's Chapter 32 is an entire methodology — Tattva Chintamani rectification, using key life events (marriage, children, career changes) to narrow the birth time. Build it as a wizard: user enters 3-5 major life events with dates, the system proposes a rectified birth time that best fits the divisional charts. This is a premium feature — astrologers charge ₹5,000-25,000 for manual rectification. No ChatGPT competitor exists.

**Remedial measures engine** (Chapter 34). Rule-based mapping from chart weaknesses to: gemstone recommendations (with weight, setting, finger, day rules), mantra prescriptions (which deity, how many repetitions), yantra recommendations, charity suggestions tied to weak planets, fasting days. Couple with a habit tracker so users log their practice — another retention hook.

**Mundane astrology module** (Chapter 35) — national charts with re-interpreted house significations (the book explicitly lists these: 1st = public health/cabinet, 3rd = telecom/media, 6th = armed forces/debt, 10th = ruler/parliament, 12th = secret enemies). Create public pages with daily mundane forecasts for major countries — excellent SEO and traffic driver for Flippa valuation.

**Muhurta (electional) engine** (Chapter 36). User specifies: "I want to start a business" or "I want to sign a marriage contract" or "launch a product" — the system scans the next N days for auspicious muhurtas based on tithi, nakshatra, yoga, karana, vara, lagna, planetary positions, and the user's natal chart. Filtered and ranked. This is a genuine utility tool with real commercial demand (Indian weddings, business launches, travel start times).

**Prashna (Horary) charts** — user asks a question, chart cast for that moment and location, rule-based analysis. Include both Krishnamurti Paddhati (KP) rules and classical Parasari rules as toggleable modes.

**Birthtime error impact visualizer** — show user how their chart changes if birth time is off by ±5, ±15, ±30 minutes. Genuinely useful and directly addresses the book's Chapter 32.

## The AI layer (done right)

This is where you differentiate from ChatGPT-wrapper competitors most dramatically.

**"Ask your chart"** — a chat interface where the LLM has structured access to the user's computed chart (all planet positions, dasa tree, ashtakavarga tables, fired yogas, etc.) as tool-callable context. When the user asks "will I get a promotion this year?" the LLM:

1. Queries the calculation engine for current Vimshottari period.
2. Checks 10th house indicators in D-1 and D-10.
3. Checks current transits to 10th house and 10th lord.
4. Retrieves relevant yogas from the fired-yoga list.
5. *Then* generates a grounded answer citing the actual data.

This is Retrieval-Augmented Generation applied to astrology. The LLM becomes an explanation layer, not a hallucination layer. Frame this prominently in marketing: "AI answers grounded in 515 pages of classical Vedic computation — not made up."

**Natural-language report generation** — daily, weekly, monthly, annual personalized forecasts delivered by email. Each section cites the astrological basis ("Based on your current Jupiter-Saturn Antardasha and Jupiter's transit through your 11th house..."). This structured citation is what signals quality to sophisticated users.
